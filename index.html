Dr. Manmohan Singh

The architect of India's economic liberalization and a symbol of integrity in public life

Here is a time line of Dr.Manmohan Singh's life

  • 1932 – Born on September 26 in Gah, Punjab (British India, now in Pakistan)
  • 1957 – Completes Economics degree at Cambridge University; later earns a D.Phil. from Oxford in 1962
  • 1966 – Joins the United Nations (UNCTAD) as an economic advisor
  • 1972 – Becomes Chief Economic Advisor to the Government of India
  • 1982 – Appointed Governor of the Reserve Bank of India
  • 1991 – Becomes Finance Minister during a major economic crisis; launches liberalization reforms that transform India’s economy
  • 1993 – Widely credited with saving India from economic collapse and initiating long-term growth
  • 2004 – Becomes the 13th Prime Minister of India and the first Sikh to hold the position
  • 2005 – Signs the India–US Civil Nuclear Agreement, marking a major shift in global diplomacy
  • 2009 – Re-elected as Prime Minister; oversees India's growth amid the global financial crisis
  • 2014 – Steps down as PM after 10 years in office, known for his integrity and economic vision
  • 2024 – Retires from active politics but remains one of India’s most respected elder statesmen

"I am not the cleverest person, but I work hard." – Dr. Manmohan Singh
